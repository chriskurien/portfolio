A Little Bit About Me

For most of my life, I had no idea what I wanted to do, so I decided to try anything and everything. I ranged from thinking I would work as a forensic accountant for the FBI (shoutout to my undergraduate Accounting & Business Technology majors and internship at the SEC) to wanting to work as a social worker for CPS (I was heavily involved in different service organizations on campus).​ Though these paths and experiences did not work out, they all had one thing in common: elements of UX. My undergraduate majors focused heavily on collaborative thought, strategic thinking, research, data analysis, and project management. Additionally, my social justice/community engagement extracurricular activities helped me further develop my interpersonal, leadership, and design skills. One day, when I spent way too much time considering what features to include in a new website I was designing for a club, I realized that I truly enjoyed understanding a user’s experience. From there, I found that there was an entire field dedicated to just that. Before finding UX, I was frustrated with my diverse experiences, wondering how I would meld them all together to be successful in one field. But now, I am grateful for them because they prepared me with the skills that I need to be successful. I am currently a Master's student at the University of Michigan studying Human-Computer Interaction with a focus on UX Research. Having finally found something I am passionate about, I am very excited to see what the future holds.

Working on Social Justice/Community Engagement Projects

I love getting involved in my local community. During my time at the University of Miami, I got the opportunity to learn more about various social issues and was able to work with different organizations from around the country. Some of my favorite experiences were:

  • Creating a poster campaign on campus to raise awareness about relationship violence.
  • Teaching different groups of students, administrators, and staff members how to create different pieces of artwork as a way to fundraise for Nicklaus Children's Hospital (think Painting with a Twist). The biggest group I taught was 60 people!
  • Serving as Program Chair for Tunnel of Oppression, a sensory-based, museum-style program designed to expose participants to various acts of oppression taking place today.
